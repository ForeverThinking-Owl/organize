# Tools, People & Organizations — The Separation of Practice Subject and Practice Sovereignty

---

## I. Two Positions Within Practice

Every practice contains two positions.

One position is responsible for **operation**: perceiving objects, understanding context, selecting tools, arranging steps, executing actions, receiving feedback, and continuing to adjust. This position can be called the **Practice Subject**.

The other position is responsible for **governance**: deciding objectives, drawing boundaries, confirming risks, adjudicating exceptions, supervising results, bearing consequences, and correcting rules. This position can be called **Practice Sovereignty**.

In simple practices, these two positions often coincide within the same person. A person picks up a tool, completes an action, and faces the result. Whoever acts, judges; whoever judges, is responsible. The person is both the operator of practice and the bearer of its ultimate consequences:

- A person cutting prey with a stone tool directly faces the result of the cut
- A person kindling fire directly bears the warmth, danger, and consequences the fire brings
- A person making a promise directly faces the relational change that follows the promise

> At this stage, Practice Subject and Practice Sovereignty coincide.

The development of complex practice gradually loosens this coincidence. Tools extend human practice capability. Organizations distribute human practice capability. AI further transforms the form of the Practice Subject. From this, a new question emerges: **when AI can undertake ever more practice operation, how should people and organizations retain Practice Sovereignty?**

---

## II. Tools Externalize Practice Capability

The emergence of tools caused practice capability to begin leaving the human body.

- **The knife** extends cutting capability
- **Fire** extends energy-transformation capability
- **Writing** extends memory capability
- **The ledger** extends accounting capability
- **The machine** extends physical-force capability
- **The computer** extends information-processing capability

Tools externalize practice methods that originally depended on the body, experience, and immediate judgment — making them preservable, repeatable, transmissible, and combinable.

> The first change tools brought was the externalization of practice capability.

People still select tools, use tools, and bear the consequences of tool use — but tools have already stabilized part of the practice method on their behalf. Stone tools stabilized cutting. Fire stabilized heating. Writing stabilized memory. Machines stabilized force. Software stabilized information processing. Human capability extends outward through tools, and practice thereby acquires greater scale and complexity.

Tools enable people to complete transformations beyond their direct bodily capability. Through tools, people can cut harder objects, move heavier things, preserve longer memories, and process more complex information. **A tool is an externalized form of a practice method.** At this point, the Practice Subject has already expanded. People act through body and tool together; part of practice capability has entered the tool; and the outcome of practice increasingly depends on the tool's stability, availability, and combinability.

> Tools extend the capability of the Practice Subject. Practice Sovereignty remains primarily borne by people. People still decide objectives, judge boundaries, and bear consequences. Tools can enhance practice, but they cannot automatically answer why practice is worth undertaking, where it must stop, or who bears the consequences.

---

## III. Organizations Distribute the Practice Subject

As practice grows more complex, the boundaries of individual skill become visible. One person can master a tool, but cannot master all tools. One person can complete a local practice, but cannot continuously complete complex social production. And so, different skills from different people are brought into collaboration.

- In medicine: diagnosis, nursing, testing, surgery, pharmacy, and administration
- In manufacturing: design, procurement, processing, quality inspection, warehousing, and delivery
- In customer service: communication, inquiry, after-sales, finance, technical support, and feedback

Every complex practice demands that different skills form collaboration around a shared object. **The organization thus emerges as the form of stable collaboration.**

Within an organization, the Practice Subject has already expanded from a single person to a collaborative structure. A complete practice is often completed by multiple people, multiple tools, and multiple skills working together. A customer complaint may be received by a communication role, have its policy judged by an after-sales role, have its refund reviewed by a finance role, have its fault diagnosed by a technical role, have its defect fixed by a development role, and finally receive a unified response from the communication role once more.

> At this point, the Practice Subject has become distributed. Practice Sovereignty, in turn, becomes structured.

Through roles, rules, permissions, approvals, supervision, and responsibility, organizations place Practice Sovereignty across different levels. Some are responsible for execution, some for review, some for approval, some for rule-making, some for bearing ultimate responsibility. The change organizations bring is this: practice operation expands from a single person to a collaborative system; Practice Sovereignty is preserved through structures of rules and responsibility.

---

## IV. AI Gives the Practice Subject a New Operational Form

The emergence of AI further transforms the Practice Subject.

AI already possesses a certain capacity for practice judgment. Within language, context, rules, and historical information, it can judge: when action is needed, where the action takes place, what kind of practice the current object belongs to, why it should be handled this way, who should bear it, and how the steps should unfold.

**This is the 5W1H of practice:**

| When | Where | What | Why | Who | How |
|------|-------|------|-----|-----|-----|
| When to practice | Where to practice | What to practice | Why to practice | Who practices | How to practice |

Previously, these judgments were primarily made by people. People identified problems, judged contexts, decided which role to invoke, chose tools, and drove the next step. After AI's emergence, these routine judgments can begin to be scheduled by language and context.

**For example, facing a single sentence:**

> The customer says the barcode scanner won't connect to the system, and they want a refund.

AI can judge that this contains multiple practice objects:

```
Device connection issue
After-sales issue
Refund risk
Customer complaint
Potential technical defect
```

It can further judge the practice path:

```
Communication role triages first
  → Technical role diagnoses the device connection
  → After-sales role judges repair/replacement/refund policy
  → Finance role reviews the refund request
  → Development role analyzes the defect if necessary
```

AI can simultaneously judge governance points:

```
Refund involved → approval required
External reply involved → confirmation required
Technical defect involved → follow-up task required
```

> AI thus begins to become an operationally meaningful Practice Subject. It can identify objects, understand context, select tools, arrange steps, trigger collaboration, receive feedback, and proceed to the next step.

The key to this change is that AI can participate in practice operation. The Practice Subject begins to further separate from the human body, human experience, and immediate human operation — entering a new form that is schedulable, executable, and feedback-receiving.

---

## V. Practice Subject and Practice Sovereignty Begin to Separate

That AI becomes a Practice Subject does not mean AI acquires Practice Sovereignty. A clear distinction is needed.

| | Practice Subject | Practice Sovereignty |
|------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Handles** | Operational questions | Governance questions |
| **Scope** | When to start, which scene to enter, what object to identify, what skill to invoke, what tool to use, how to execute, how to feed back, how to proceed to the next step | Whether the objective is correct, whether the boundary is clear, whether the risk is acceptable, how exceptions are adjudicated, who is responsible for results, how rules are corrected |

AI can undertake ever more practice scheduling. It can judge when, where, what, why, who, and how to practice. It can invoke tools, generate action paths, coordinate different roles, and output structured results. People and organizations retain Practice Sovereignty. Objectives, boundaries, exceptions, responsibility, and value judgments must still be borne by people and organizations.

> **AI acquires Practice Scheduling Authority. People and organizations retain Practice Sovereignty.**
>
> **Practice Subject and Practice Sovereignty begin to separate.**

This separation does not weaken the human position — it transforms it. People shift upward from vast amounts of routine execution toward:

```
Goal-setting → Boundary-defining → Risk-approving → Exception-adjudicating → Rule-correcting → Ultimate responsibility
```

> AI handles how practice runs. People decide why practice is worth undertaking, where it stops, and who bears responsibility.

---

## VI. Approval Is the Minimal Mechanism of Practice Sovereignty

Approval is often understood as a workflow node. Within an AI practice system, it carries a deeper meaning.

> **Approval is the minimal mechanism of Practice Sovereignty.**

When an AI prepares to execute a high-risk action, practice must pause and await sovereign confirmation. For example:

- Whether to permit creating a refund request
- Whether to permit sending an external reply
- Whether to permit modifying an order
- Whether to permit submitting a quotation
- Whether to permit deploying code
- Whether to permit writing to a financial system

Once executed, these actions enter social production and produce consequences. **They require sovereign confirmation.**

Approval is not simply "approve" or "reject." It contains at least five capabilities:

1. **Confirming purpose**
2. **Constraining boundaries**
3. **Modifying parameters**
4. **Requesting additional information**
5. **Bearing responsibility**

Thus, a complete approval may take forms such as:

```
Approve execution
Reject execution
Approve with modified amount
Release with redacted results
Add accountability comment
Escalate to a higher level
Request supplementary evidence
```

> Approval brings the AI's Practice Subjecthood into the boundary of human sovereignty. It transforms "executable" into "authorized to execute," and "system action" into "accountable practice." The meaning of approval lies in confirming boundaries, accepting risk, and recording responsibility.

---

## VII. Supervision Is the Continuous Form of Practice Sovereignty

Approval is point-like. Supervision is continuous.

Practice Sovereignty cannot appear only at high-risk nodes. After AI participates in practice, the operational process must be observable, traceable, reviewable, and correctable. Otherwise, people nominally retain sovereignty but cannot understand how the system acts, nor effectively bear responsibility.

**Supervision requires recording the practice process:**

```
What was the input
What context did the system see
What skill was invoked
What tool was selected
What were the parameters
How was risk judged
How did approval occur
What was the result
What experience was accumulated
What rules need correction
```

**Supervision also requires feedback.** The result of a single practice should not remain only in a log entry. It should be organized into experience, becoming a condition for the next practice. For example:

- A certain category of refund requests frequently requires financial review
- A certain category of technical faults frequently leads to customer complaints
- A certain actor frequently generates boundary-crossing replies
- A certain category of tool results requires redaction before release
- A certain rule needs its boundaries reset

Once this experience enters organizational memory, the practice system can gradually improve.

> Supervision is not post-hoc inspection. Supervision is the continuous form of Practice Sovereignty. It enables people and organizations to continuously observe the AI Practice Subject, and through feedback, memory, and rule correction, make the system more reliable.

---

## VIII. Responsibility Is the Ultimate Form of Practice Sovereignty

Practice must ultimately face consequences.

- A wrong refund → impacts funds
- A wrong reply → impacts customer relationships
- A wrong diagnosis → impacts treatment
- A wrong deployment → impacts system stability
- A wrong approval → impacts organizational trust

AI can execute. AI can judge. AI can schedule. But the consequences of social practice require a responsibility structure to receive them.

Responsibility answers:

```
Who set the objective?
Who authorized the boundary?
Who approved the risk?
Who accepted the result?
Who corrected the rule?
Who faces the external consequences?
```

> Autonomous operation that enters a responsibility structure can become governable practice. Without it, it is merely automation.

**Responsibility is the ultimate form of Practice Sovereignty.**

- **Approval** confirms risk
- **Supervision** observes process
- **Responsibility** bears consequences

The three together constitute the sovereignty structure within an AI practice system. This means every critical practice must leave traces of responsibility: who proposed the objective, who confirmed the boundary, who approved the risk, who accepted the result, who bears the consequence. Only then can autonomous operation enter social production without becoming an uncontrolled chain of actions.

---

## IX. The Human Position Shifts Upward

After AI enters practice, the human position changes.

Previously, people had to personally complete vast amounts of routine judgment and execution: identifying problems, searching for information, selecting tools, driving processes, chasing collaboration, organizing results. Now, these routine practices can gradually be handed over to AI scheduling.

The human position shifts upward. People withdraw from repetitive execution and enter the positions of objectives, boundaries, exceptions, and responsibility:

| Past (Executor) | Future (Governor) |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Identifying problems | Setting practice objectives |
| Searching for information | Defining practice boundaries |
| Driving processes | Confirming high-risk actions |
| Chasing collaboration | Handling exceptional situations |
| Organizing results | Supervising operational outcomes |
| — | Correcting rules for the next practice |

Routine practice is scheduled by AI. Critical practice is confirmed by people. Exceptional practice is adjudicated by people. Ultimate consequences are borne by people and organizations. This transformation is the new structure that follows the separation of Practice Subject and Practice Sovereignty.

---

## X. Who Practices, Who Is Responsible

The core change of the AI era is that **Practice Subject and Practice Sovereignty begin to separate**.

- **Practice Subject** answers "who operates"
- **Practice Sovereignty** answers "who decides, who stops, who bears responsibility"

AI can progressively undertake the role of Practice Subject in the operational sense. It can identify objects, judge contexts, select tools, arrange steps, schedule collaboration, receive feedback, and drive routine practice toward autonomous operation.

People and organizations retain Practice Sovereignty in the normative sense. Objectives, boundaries, exceptions, approval, supervision, and responsibility must still be borne by people and organizations.

```
The old question: How do people use tools to complete practice?
The AI-era question: How does AI undertake practice operation while people retain Practice Sovereignty?
```

> The Practice Subject handles how to operate.
> Practice Sovereignty handles why it is worth doing, where to stop, and who bears responsibility.

> **AI takes over practice scheduling. People retain Practice Sovereignty.**
>
> **This is the new division of labor among tools, people, and organizations in the AI era.**
